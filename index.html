Voter Registration

Learn how to register as a voter, required documents, and online/offline methods.

How to Register

  • Online: Visit voters.eci.gov.in - Fill Form 6
  • App: Download Voter Helpline App
  • Offline: Visit nearest Electoral Registration Office

Documents Needed

  • Age proof (birth certificate, Class 10 marksheet)
  • Address proof (Aadhaar, ration card, utility bill)
  • Passport-size photograph

Tip: Processing takes 15-30 days. Apply well before elections!

Who Can Vote?

Eligibility criteria, special provisions for NRIs, service voters, and persons with disabilities.

Basic Requirements

  • Indian citizen aged 18+
  • Resident of the constituency
  • Not disqualified by law

Special Categories

  • NRIs: Can register as overseas electors
  • Armed Forces: Vote via postal ballot
  • PwD: Assistance available at booths

EVM & VVPAT

How Electronic Voting Machines work, security features, and the paper trail system.

EVM Components

  • Control Unit: With presiding officer
  • Ballot Unit: Voter presses candidate button
  • VVPAT: Prints paper slip (visible 7 sec)

Security Features

  • No internet, WiFi, or Bluetooth
  • One-time programmable chip
  • Randomized booth allocation
  • Mock poll before voting begins

Voting Day

Step-by-step guide for what happens at the polling station on election day.

At the Polling Station

  1. Queue up in the voter line
  2. Show Voter ID for verification
  3. Indelible ink on left index finger
  4. Enter screened compartment
  5. Press button next to your candidate
  6. Verify VVPAT slip (7 seconds)
  7. Exit the polling station

Remember

  • No phones inside voting compartment
  • Voting hours: 7 AM - 6 PM
  • Voting is completely FREE

Model Code of Conduct

Rules that political parties and candidates must follow during election season.

Key Rules

  • No new government schemes after announcement
  • No misuse of government resources
  • No appeals to caste or religion
  • Campaign silence 48 hours before polling
  • No paid news or fake information

Enforcement

ECI monitors via flying squads, surveillance teams, and social media monitoring cells.

Results & Counting

How votes are counted, results declared, and government formation happens.

Counting Process

  1. Postal ballots counted first
  2. EVM results — round by round
  3. VVPAT verification (5 random booths)
  4. Real-time trends on ECI website
  5. Final result by Returning Officer

Key Numbers

  • 272 seats = Majority in Lok Sabha
  • FPTP: Highest votes wins (no runoff)
